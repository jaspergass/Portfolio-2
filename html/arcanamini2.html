Arcana Mini App 2

Fall 2023 - Spring 2024

Introduction

This was a team project organized through a club called Surge. I was the team leader as well as the only designer on the team. Working with 4 developers, our goal was to produce a functioning mobile app using React Native. We decided to work on an app concept I previously created called Arcana Mini →. In the end, we were unable to complete the app, but I was able to finish a complete re-design of the app and a functional figma prototype. The link to the finished prototype can be found here →.

Process

Just like the previous Arcana Mini, this mobile app acts as a journal and guidebook companion for tarot reading. Tarot reading is a spiritual practice that uses a physical deck of cards to gain insight into the past, present or future. The app allows users to look up definitions of cards and journal their thoughts to document their tarot journeys and promote reflection. The app requires that the user owns their own deck of tarot cards, but otherwise is accessible to both novice and expert tarot readers.

Originally, I wanted to use the same design as the previous app prototype and focus on the coding. After recieving some critiques, I realized that I should take the opportunity to improve the design of the app. This was a very long process for me because it was the first time I had ever designed something on my own of this size. Below you can see two iterations of designs that I went though. The left side has a very unqiue and boxy style that I was very drawn to, but ultimately it didn't suit the app's personality. The right side shows where I started to refine a more soft and modern look that would make the app much more approachable and calm. One of the biggest things I learned through the critiques was that, because journaling can be very personal, it was important for the app to feel safe and trustworthy through its design.

Since I was re-designing the app, I also took the time to consider some new features. Two things I considered were a section to rank your mood in each journal entry, and a way to manually enter the cards you pulled in each reading. This would allow us to show the user statistics of their mood and most frequent cards over time. These features could be very useful because it would reveal patterns in mood and themes in their tarot readings, which should encourage reflection and insight. Below are some iterations of these features. In the end, I never fully polished these features because my team decided it would be outside the scope of the project.

An important step to creating the app was understanding the flow and technical requirements. We chose to visualize this using a site map created in FigJam. This clearly shows all the screens that the app would need and how they are linked. This also helped inform the structure of the final figma prototype.

Challenges

This project was unfortunately subject to many challenges. One of the biggest challenges was working with React Native. All of our developers were new to React Native and were learning it for this project alone. We chose React Native because we wanted to launch the app for both android and IOS systems and because it would be a good learning opportunity. However, this ended up being too much for the developers to take on and was a significant barrier to the progress of the app.

Another key challenge was communication. I learned early on that there are very few team projects in the computer science program, so the developers had little to no experience doing team work. This meant that most of them had a very hard time communicating and keeping up with the team, and I would have to step up to being the team leader due to my experience and my involvement with the app concept. It also prevented them from understanding the scope of the project. With more team experience, I knew from the beginning how much work was going to be involved with a project of this size and I was willing to sacrifice my time working on it. The other members of the team struggled to calculate how much time was needed and were quickly overwhelmed with the work. I think this could have been avoided by choosing a more simple project, but at the same time I think it was still worth the experience to try and make this project work.

Reflection

In the end, we were unable to create an app in React Native, but I did complete a full working prototype in Figma. Despite the challenges, I learned a lot working on this design. I was working full time as a UI designer over the fall semester and doing the design work for this project after hours, which meant I was spending a lot of time on Figma. I was learning so many new techniques all the time and solidifying the knowledge by practicing actively at work and through this project. After this project, my knowledge of Figma has increased tenfold and I have a new sense of confidence which will allow me to keep creating and learning.
